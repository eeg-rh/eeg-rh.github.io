We're looking for participants for an EEG study!

You'll get to experience an interesting neuroscience experiment, help us to research emotion processing, and have a chance to win a Â£25 Amazon voucher.

Participants must be aged 18-24, neurotypical, and right-handed, as these characteristics have all been found to influence the brain activity we're studying.

The study takes 1 hour 30 minutes and is to investigate the links between social connections and emotion processing. EEG is a completely safe procedure which measures the naturally-occuring electrical signals from your brain.

Please email zfac029@live.rhul.ac.uk if you'd like to take part or have any questions - or alternatively, submit you email via this form and someone will be in touch.

What to expect:

  • The study takes place in the EEG lab (Wolfson 117) - we can meet you at the Wolfson building entrance if you're not sure where that is.
  • First, you'll read an information sheet and we will tell you about the EEG procedure.
  • The EEG cap and electrodes will be placed on your head. A few electrodes will also be positioned on your face to record blinking and eye movement. This preparation will take around 30-40 minutes as it involves putting lots of conductive gel dots and electrodes onto the cap.
  • You'll be asked to complete a series of simple tasks about judging emotion, which will last around 30 minutes.
  • We'll remove the EEG cap and ask you to fill out a questionnaire about social connectness, which will last around 10-20 minutes.
  • Finally, if you'd like to wash the conductive gel out of your hair, you'll be shown to a sink with shampoo and a dryer.
